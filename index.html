Pubs

Don't know where to go when the restrictions end? Try these pubs below and you will not be disappointed!

The Skeff

Just beside Eyre Square, The Skeff Bar is one of Galway's most popular pubs. The Skeff has been around since 1850 and offer the finest pints, cocktails and whiskeys from all over the world. With live music seven nights a week The Skeff is a must visit establishment. Come see the unique interior of the pub and sample some of their finest food the kicthen provide. Make sure that the Skeff is your first stop.

How can I find the Skeff?

Simply click directions on the map, this will bring you to Google Maps, from there all you need to do is follow the directions and you will be on your way to a good night in The Skeff.

An Púcán

An Púcán, just a 2 minute walk from Eyre Square is a very popular pub among the locals and students alike. Some of Galway's best live music acts can be found every evening at An Púcán with local music acts performing in the front bar, followed by exciting line ups for the stage in the back bar to keep the momentum going throughout the night. With multiple Television's in the back bar, An Púcán is the pub to go to during a Champions League night or a Premier League weekend. Come to An Púcán and have a bite to eat, a pint, and the bit of craic.

How can I find An Púcán?

Simply click directions on the map, this will bring you to Google Maps, from there all you need to do is follow the directions and you will be on your way to a good night in An Púcán.

The Hole In The Wall

The Hole In The Wall, is a hidden gem in Galway. The Hole In The Wall has the look of a classic pub, with it's thatched rood it shows the history of a classic pub. The pub got it's name due to the fact that there was a hole in the back garden of the pub which adjoined to back of the old Garda Station, Gardaí would slip through the hole on the sly for a quick pint. Although the hole and the Garda station are gone the name lives on. When entering the pub you would think your gone back in time with it's flagstone floor and sturdy furniture. With it's good value drink The Hole in the Wall is a must visit.

How can I find The Hole In The Wall?

Simply click directions on the map, this will bring you to Google Maps, from there all you need to do is follow the directions and you will be on your way to some classic pints in The Hole In The Wall.

Are you up for a challenge?

There is no better way to say goodbye to the restrictions than doing a pub crawl to make up for all the months we've had to endure. On the map you will see that it contains what we think are the most popular pubs in Galway, the map has a start point at A and an end point. Start at An Púcán and crawl around to each pub at your own pace. We are not telling you to do every pub on the map in one day, just have some craic.
